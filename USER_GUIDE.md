Grow & Tell is a garden planning and reflection app designed around real home-gardening workflows. This guide explains how to use each section of the app and how the pieces fit together.

## Getting Started

Grow & Tell does not require an account or sign-in.

From the landing page, select Start Planning or Dashboard to enter the dashboard. All data is stored locally in your browser, so progress is saved automatically on the same device.

## Dashboard Overview

The dashboard is the central hub of the app. It provides quick access to all garden areas and high-level summaries, including:

- Beds
- Plantings
- Tasks
- Harvests
- Soil

Each section uses card-based layouts and modal interactions to keep the experience focused and easy to scan.

Beds act as the foundation for plantings and soil records.

## Plantings

Plantings track what is currently growing in each bed.

## What You Can Do

- View planting entries in a card-based layout
- Filter and scan history to see what was planted and when

Adding crops happens inside the Beds feature. The Plant Log is where you review planting activity.

## Tasks

Tasks help you plan and track ongoing garden work.

## What You Can Do

- Default tasks provide common seasonal and maintenance activities
- Custom tasks let you add your own one-time or recurring work
- Mark tasks as complete
- Edit or delete tasks
- View task status at a glance

Tasks are designed to support real maintenance routines without over-automation.

## What You Can Do

- Log harvested crops with quantity and unit

- View totals and summaries

Harvest data feeds into the Plant Log, giving you a historical view of what your garden has produced over time.

## Soil Records

Soil tracking is focused on clarity and practicality.

- Each bed can have one soil record
- Soil records include:
- Amendments (such as compost or nutrients)
- Soil test (optional)

## Each test includes:

- pH
- Nitrogen (N)
- Phosphorus (P)
- Potassium (K)
- Date tested

Only the latest test is shown on the soil card

## Soil Summary

- The dashboard summary shows:
- How many beds have been tested
- Which nutrients are currently deficient
- Nutrient status is derived from test values rather than manually entered labels

This design reflects real gardening behavior, where testing is occasional and decisions are made based on current conditions.

## Data Storage

- All data is stored locally in your browser using local storage.
- No login required
- No external services
- Data persists between sessions on the same device
- Clearing browser storage will reset the app
